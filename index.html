LAHORE: TUESDAY, 17 December 1971 VOL. XXIV • NO. 82 PRICE 25 PAISA

INDIAN ARMY ENTERS DHAKA

Major Developments in the War

Original front page - 23 March 1971

Complete edition available at Nawa-e-Waqt office

THE WAR CONTINUES

Joint forces advance towards Dhaka. Pakistani forces offer resistance in multiple sectors. Heavy fighting reported on the outskirts.

  • Indian paratroopers land near Tangail
  • Pakistani forces retreat from forward positions
  • Civilians celebrate in liberated areas
  • International media covers the offensive

Casualties: 127 reported dead

Operations near Dhaka - 23 March

Pakistani forces conducting defensive operations

POLITICAL IMPACT

International pressure mounts for peace talks. UN schedules emergency session for March 24.

US State Dept: Expresses deep concern

Soviet Union: Offers mediation

FINAL STAGES

Dhaka surrounded from three sides. Supply lines severely disrupted.

  1. Meghna River crossed - March 20
  2. Air strikes on key positions - March 21
  3. Governor's House surrounded - March 22
  4. Radio station captured - March 23

City expected to fall within 48 hours

Military map of East Pakistan

Red markers indicate Indian positions

BATTLE ANALYSIS

Coordinated air, land, and sea operations by Indian forces. Pakistani troops demonstrate tactical brilliance but face numerical disadvantage of 3:1.

UN calls for immediate ceasefire. Security Council emergency session scheduled.

International analysts predict prolonged conflict if ceasefire not achieved.

KEY EVENTS:

  1. March 20: Cross Meghna River under cover
  2. March 21: Precision air strikes on GHQ
  3. March 22: Governor's House surrounded
  4. March 23: Radio station captured
  5. March 23: Curfew imposed in Dhaka

PAKISTANI FORCES HUMILIATE INDIAN ARMY

In a counter-attack near Sylhet, Pakistani forces captured 200+ Indian soldiers including 3 officers. Enemy forces retreated in disarray abandoning heavy weaponry.

  • 206 soldiers captured including 3 officers
  • 15 tanks and 50 vehicles seized
  • Large cache of ammunition recovered
  • Sylhet sector completely cleared

"Enemy came with dreams of victory, will return with tales of defeat" - Military Spokesperson

RELIEF EFFORTS

Army establishes field hospitals treating 500+ civilians.

Relief Efforts:

  • 3 field hospitals established
  • 2000 civilians provided food
  • 5 mosques protected
  • Relief camps for displaced

PAKISTAN TO CONTINUE AID

Prime Minister announces continued financial and military aid to East Pakistan. "We stand with our brothers in this difficult time," he stated in national address.

Financial Package:

  • Rs. 50 crore approved
  • Food supplies dispatched
  • Medical aid included

Military Support:

  • Additional troops sent
  • Ammunition supplies
  • Air support intensified

CHINA REAFFIRMS SUPPORT

Chinese Foreign Minister declares full support for Pakistan's territorial integrity. "We stand with our all-weather friend," he said in Beijing press conference.

Chinese Statement Highlights:

  • Condemn external interference
  • Support UN resolution
  • Offer diplomatic assistance
  • Economic aid ongoing

REFUGEE CRISIS

Thousands cross border into India daily. Aid agencies struggle to provide relief.

Refugee count: 50,000+

Camps: 12 established

Red Cross: Emergency aid

UNHCR: Monitoring

ECONOMIC IMPACT

Markets closed indefinitely. Food prices rising 40% in affected areas.

Price Comparison:
Wheat: +35% Rice: +42%
Flour: +30% Oil: +25%

CULTURAL NOTES

Poets and writers document wartime experiences. New literature emerges from crisis.

  • Faiz Ahmed Faiz: New poem "Dhaka"
  • Art exhibition in Karachi
  • War correspondents publish diaries

Upcoming: Poetry reading at Alhamra, March 25
